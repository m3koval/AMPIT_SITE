AI Phishing Is Getting Better. Your Verification Habits Need to Get Better Too.

Good grammar is no longer a safety signal.

AMP IT · Charlotte, NCJuly 8, 20265 min read
VERIFY

The risk

AI can make scam emails sound polished, familiar, and calm enough to slip past the old warning signs your team was trained to notice.

Why it matters

Professional firms handle information other people trust them to protect. One convincing message can become a compromised mailbox, fraudulent payment, exposed client data, or insurance headache.

Practical next steps

  1. Treat urgency as a warning, not a command.
  2. Verify money, credential, code, or bank-change requests outside the message.
  3. Use a known phone number, trusted Teams chat, or the vendor site opened directly.
  4. Do not use links, phone numbers, or attachments inside the suspicious email.
  5. Report clicks quickly so credentials, mailbox rules, and sign-in activity can be checked.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AI phishing?
AI phishing uses generative AI to create more convincing scam messages that can sound natural, professional, and personalized.
How can employees spot AI phishing?
Focus less on grammar and more on the request: urgency, payment changes, credential requests, verification codes, and links that require login.
What should a business do if someone clicked?
Report quickly, reset credentials, review sign-in activity, and check mailbox forwarding rules.
