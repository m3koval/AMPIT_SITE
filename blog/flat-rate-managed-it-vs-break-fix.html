Break-fix IT feels cheaper because you only pay when something hurts. The problem is that downtime, risk, and surprise invoices usually cost more than the ticket.

Break-Fix Rewards Delay

In a break-fix model, the IT provider gets called after the pain shows up. Preventive work feels optional until the failure becomes urgent. Patching waits, backups are assumed, old accounts remain open, and documentation lives in one person’s head.

Managed IT Changes The Rhythm

Flat-rate managed IT shifts the work from reaction to stewardship. The goal is not more tickets. The goal is fewer surprises: proactive maintenance, security ownership, predictable cost, and faster support from a team that already knows your environment.

When Break-Fix Might Still Fit

Break-fix can still work for a very small, low-risk business with simple systems and no sensitive client data. Law firms, financial firms, CPA practices, insurance agencies, and growing companies usually outgrow it quickly.

Questions To Ask

Who owns security when nothing is broken? Are backups tested? What happens when an employee leaves? How fast can support understand the environment? Can the owner stop being the IT coordinator?
