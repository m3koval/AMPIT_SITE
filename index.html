AI Risk

Is Shadow AI leaking your client data?

When employees use public AI tools — ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini — without oversight, confidential client data can leave your business silently. No breach notification. No audit trail. No way to know it happened.

For law firms, CPAs, financial advisors, and insurance agencies, that exposure isn't just a security risk — it's a liability, a compliance failure, and a malpractice issue.

Managed IT isn't just about keeping computers running. It's about knowing what's happening across your environment — including how AI is being used — before it becomes a problem.

Book a Free Assessment Explore our full AI Governance program →
01
Unmanaged AI tool usage
Employees paste client documents, financial data, or case notes into public AI models with no visibility or control at the IT level.
02
No policy, no documentation
Without a documented AI governance policy, you have no defensible position with a malpractice carrier, regulator, or client asking how their data was handled.
03
The risk is already inside your firm
Shadow AI doesn't come from outside attackers — it comes from well-meaning employees trying to work faster. Standard endpoint security doesn't catch it.
04
Regulators and insurers are catching up fast
State bar associations, FINRA, HIPAA auditors, and cyber liability carriers are all beginning to ask directly about AI use policies. Even if your industry isn't regulated, your clients and insurers may require it. Being unprepared is no longer a defensible answer.
